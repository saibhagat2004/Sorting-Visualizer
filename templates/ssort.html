Selection Sorting

Selection sort is a simple and efficient sorting algorithm that works by repeatedly selecting the smallest (or largest) element from the unsorted portion of the list and moving it to the sorted portion of the list. Although simple to understand, it is not the most efficient sorting algorithm for large datasets.

In Selection Sort algorithm,

  • The entire list is considered unsorted initially.
  • In each iteration, the algorithm identifies the smallest (or largest) element in the unsorted portion.
  • The selected element is swapped with the first element of the unsorted part.
  • This process is repeated for the remaining unsorted portion until the entire list is sorted.

  • Total no. of passes: n-1

  • Total no. of comparisons: n*(n-1)/2
